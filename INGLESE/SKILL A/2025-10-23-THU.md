#### Talking about past

When talking about past, there are more tenses and forms that can be used, from past simple, to past perfect, from simple to continuous etc...
When forming past simple, if the last sound is a wovel or a voiced consonat, the "*ed*" is pronounced /d/, while if it is a voiceless consonant,  it is pronounced /t/.

Forming tenses:
- past simple: *-ed*
- past continuous: past simple of *to have* + present participle
- past perfect simple: present simple of *to have* + past participle
- past perfect continuous: present simple of *to have* + past participle of *to be* + present participle
- past simple passive: past simple of *to be* + past participle
- past continuous passive: past simple of *to be* + present participle of *to be* + past participle
- past perfects passive: have + past participle of *to be* + past participle

When talking about past, "***to be used to***" and "***would***" are options, even if "***would***" can be used only with actions, and not verbs such as *be*, *have*, *like*.
For example: "*I used to watch tv as a kid.*" and "*I would watch tv as a kid*." are both correct, while between "*I used to be an actor some years ago*." and "~~*I would be an actor some years ago.*~~" only the first one is correct.
