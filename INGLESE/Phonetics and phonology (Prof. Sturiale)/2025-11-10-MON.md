#### Strong ad weak vowels

Vowels in english can be divided into strong and weak vowels.
All vowels, except schwa /ə/ can occur in stressed syllables, however only a small number of vowels usually does.
The vowels in ***stressed*** syllables are called ***strong***, while those in ***unstressed*** syllables are called ***weak***.

Every vowel grapheme can be a schwa /*ə*/.
Schwa /*ə*/ is always unstressed.

Every time there is a ***silent*** letter, the previous ***vowel*** sound is ***long***.

#### The standard lexical set

The standard lexical set has been presented for the first time by John Wells in his book *Accents of English - Cambridge University Press*, in 1982.
Wells classified English words in 24 lexical sets, with a keyword name, the RP and GenAm (*general american*).
